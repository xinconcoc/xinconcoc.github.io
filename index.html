About Me

I earned my Bachelor’s degree in Control and Automation from Da Nang University of Science and Technology (DUT) in 2022. After graduation, I received a scholarship to pursue a Master’s degree in Computer Information Engineering at Korea National University of Transportation (KNUT) under the supervision of Professor Kim Yong-Hwa. My research during this time focused on applying semi-supervised and self-supervised learning algorithms, incorporating contrastive learning and complementary labeling, to fault diagnosis in power systems.

In 2025, I completed my Master’s degree and was awarded a scholarship to pursue a PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Michigan-Dearborn (UM) under the supervision of Associate Professor Bui Van Hai and Professor Wencong Su. My current research focuses on the applications of machine learning in the optimal operation of power systems, particularly in optimizing power flow (OPF). I am exploring advanced techniques, including combined CNN-LSTM models with attention mechanisms, graph neural networks, and reinforcement learning integrated with physics-informed approaches.

I am deeply passionate about leveraging cutting-edge machine learning techniques to enhance the reliability, efficiency, and sustainability of modern power systems.
