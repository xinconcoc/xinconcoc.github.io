About Me

I earned my Bachelor’s degree in Control and Automation in Da Nang University of Science and Technology in 2022. After graduation, I received a scholarship to pursue a Master’s degree in Computer Information Engineering in Korea National University of Transportation under the supervision of Professor Kim Yong-Hwa. My research during this time focused on applying semi-supervised and self-supervised learning algorithms with contrastive learning and complementary labeling to fault diagnosis in power systems. In 2025, I completed my Master’s degree and was awarded a scholarship to pursue a PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering in the University of Michigan-Dearborn. My current research focuses on the applications of machine learning in optimal operation of power systems, particularly in optimizing power flow (OPF). I am exploring advanced techniques, including combined CNN-LSTM models with attention mechanisms, graph neural networks, and reinforcement learning integrated with physics-informed approaches.
